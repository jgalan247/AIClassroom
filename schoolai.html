üì§ Example Output (Topic: Electrolysis):

üìö Simple Explanation:
Electrolysis is using electricity to break apart compounds. Think of it like using electric energy to "split up" substances that are stuck together.

Here's what happens:
1. You dissolve or melt a compound (called an electrolyte)
2. You put in two electrodes (metal rods) connected to a battery
3. The electricity makes ions move: positive ions go to the negative electrode, negative ions go to the positive electrode
4. When ions reach the electrodes, they gain or lose electrons and turn back into atoms

üí° Helpful Analogy:
Imagine a school dance where boys and girls are holding hands (bonded). Then the teachers (electricity) tell all boys to go to one side and all girls to the other - they separate! That's basically electrolysis.
