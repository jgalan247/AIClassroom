üí° Tips for Effective Differentiation

  • Same Learning Goal: All levels should aim for the same objective, just with different support
  • Multiple Entry Points: Offer choice so students can select appropriate challenge level
  • Flexible Grouping: Students might need different levels for different tasks
  • Build Independence: Gradually reduce scaffolding as students gain confidence
  • Vocabulary is Key: Often the main barrier is language, not understanding
  • Visual Support: Add diagrams, charts, or images to support all learners
  • Check Understanding: Regularly assess if students are at the right level
  • Avoid Stereotypes: Lower reading age doesn't mean lower intelligence
